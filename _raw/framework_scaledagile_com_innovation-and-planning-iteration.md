Innovation and Planning Iteration

100% utilization drives unpredictability.

– Don Reinertsen

Definition: The Innovation and Planning (IP) Iteration is a unique, dedicated iteration that occurs every PI. It provides an estimating buffer for meeting PI Objectives and dedicated time for innovation, continuing education, PI Planning, and Inspect and Adapt (I&A) events.

Summary

The Innovation and Planning (IP) iteration is an important part of the planning cadence for Agile Teams and ARTs. The IP iteration is essential for sustaining flow, allowing teams to focus on creativity and problem-solving. It encourages learning and innovation through activities like hackathons and the formation of Communities of Practice. IP iterations help Agile Teams acquire new skills, focus on improvements, and share knowledge. Additionally, conducting Inspect and Adapt (I&A) and PI Planning events during this time maintains a steady flow of value delivery through the products and solutions the Agile Teams develop.

What is the Innovation and Planning iteration?

The IP iteration is the last iteration in a PI. During this iteration, teams reset, realign, and generate focus for the coming PI. In SAFe, the goal is to keep delivering value to customers. However, focusing solely on immediate tasks can sometimes limit the team's chances to innovate and learn. For this reason, no work is planned into the IP Iteration during PI Planning, but rather, the time is reserved to ensure that Agile Teams have the space they need to be creative while still getting their work done.

The activities during the IP iterations include innovation, learning, evaluating what has been done, and preparing for PI Planning. IP iterations help teams stay on track to meet their goals and improve their performance. This leads to happier employees and less burnout. IP iterations also make it easier for organizations to adapt quickly to changes in the market. As a result, companies can improve their competitive edge and stay ahead in the market.

Inspect and Adapt, and PI Planning

Holding the Inspect and Adapt (I&A) and PI planning during the IP iteration helps maintain the pace of regular work. These events are scheduled well beforehand, ensuring appropriate preparation can take place. Also, last-minute refinements of the Agile Release Train (ART) backlogs and detailing features often take place during the IP Iteration, which can improve the productivity of the next planning session.

The PI System Demo

The PI System Demo occurs at the end of each PI, as part of the I&A event. It is the integrated presentation of the work of all teams on the train, done in a staging environment to mimic production as closely as possible. For ARTs that are part of a Solution Train, the PI system demo feeds into the aggregate Solution Demo, which also takes place during the IP iteration. These events are a more structured and formal process to demonstrate all the features and capabilities developed throughout the entire PI for a solution train.

When a solution includes hardware (and other components), it's harder to integrate end-to-end continuously. Full integration may be doable only during the IP iteration, so it makes sense to plan for that.

What are the other benefits of the IP iteration?

Addressing Improvement Opportunities

Continuous delivery increases demands on a modern and responsive development infrastructure. This requires new tools and systems, such as continuous integration environments and test automation frameworks. Teams also need to adopt new tools and address opportunities to improve processes. Because they are highly dependent on their working environments, it's often more efficient to improve infrastructure or perform a migration to a new tool or system when the teams aren't in the midst of critical work. The IP Iteration is a great time to align across teams on those improvements and more formally prioritize them across the ART.

Increasing Predictability

Lean thinking recognizes that "100 percent utilization drives unpredictable results." [1] Encouraging planning to full capacity prevents teams from having the ability to make adjustments when problems occur. This causes volatility and delays. As a remedy, the IP iteration offers a 'guard band' (or buffer) to prevent unfinished work from the current PI from carrying over to the next PI.

During PI planning, the ART does not plan features or stories for the IP iteration, providing a buffer for the teams to adapt to unexpected events. This can offset delays from dependencies, and other issues, increasing the likelihood of meeting Team and ART PI Objectives. Enhancing the predictability of achieving outcomes is vital to the business. However, routinely using the IP Iteration to complete work can become a failure pattern since this defeats its primary purpose, and innovation will probably suffer. Teams and ART leadership must ensure that this estimating guard band is respected.

Investing in Innovation and Learning

Innovation and learning are part of SAFe's continuous learning culture. As technology and methods change, employees need to learn new skills to adopt them. However, tight deadlines often limit time for innovation and professional growth. That's where IP iterations come in—they provide dedicated time for learning and developing new skills and forming Communities of Practice (CoPs) to share knowledge.

Hackathons encourage creativity and innovation by allowing team members to work on their own ideas. The best part is that many of these ideas can benefit the company over time. Each hackathon follows two simple rules: 1. People can work on any project with anyone if it fits the company's mission. 2. Teams present their work at the end of the event.

Key Takeaways
The IP Iteration occurs at the end of every PI and provides time for creativity and problem-solving.
The IP Iteration is an opportunity for Agile Teams to innovate and develop their skills.
Holding the I&A and PI planning during IP iterations avoids disrupting the flow of work.
