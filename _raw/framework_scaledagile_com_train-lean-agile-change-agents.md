# Train Lean-Agile Change Agents

* * *

This article is article **two** in the **SAFe® Implementation Roadmap** **series.** [Click here](https://framework.scaledagile.com/implementation-roadmap/) to view the entire roadmap.

* * *

[Reaching the Tipping Point](https://framework.scaledagile.com/reaching-the-tipping-point/) describes that the need to adopt new practices for business agility and solution development is often driven by a burning platform—a problem too severe to solve using the enterprise's current way of working. It creates the level of urgency needed to inspire significant change.

However, even if that is not the case, as the pace of technology advancements, market changes, and digital disruption reshapes the modern business model, this sense of urgency has become the new norm. Now more than ever, the ability to substantially improve development practices to enable more significant innovation, higher quality, and faster time-to-market is the key to success. Change is at hand. For those following the proven critical moves identified in the SAFe [Implementation Roadmap](https://framework.scaledagile.com/implementation-roadmap/), this article describes the second step in that series: train Lean-Agile change agents.

# Details

## The Need for a Powerful Coalition

Once an organization reaches its tipping point and the rationale for a significant change becomes evident, the arduous journey begins. In Accelerate _, _ Kotter discusses eight stages for guiding organizational transformation and what it takes to make it stick \[1\].

1. Create a sense of urgency
2. Build a guiding coalition
3. Form a strategic vision
4. Enlist a volunteer army
5. Enable action by removing barriers
6. Generate short-term wins
7. Sustain acceleration
8. Institute change

Step 2 needs a 'sufficiently powerful guiding coalition' of stakeholders. An effective coalition requires the following:

- Leaders who can set the vision, show the way, and remove impediments to change
- Practitioners, managers, and change agents who can implement specific process changes
- Sufficient organizational credibility to be taken seriously
- The expertise needed to make fast, intelligent decisions

Experience shows that the organization must take four critical steps to create a coalition that is sufficiently powerful to initiate this change:

1. Train Lean-Agile change agents as Certified SAFe® Practice Consultants (SPCs)
2. Create a Lean-Agile Center of Excellence (LACE)
3. Train executives, managers, and other leaders
4. Enlist leaders to engage and 'lead the change' of implementing SAFe

## Develop SPCs as Change Agents

In most enterprises, the primary SAFe change agents appear as certified SPCs. Sourced internally and externally, they come from many sources, including trusted consulting partners, internal business and technology leaders, portfolio/program/project managers, and trusted individual contributors with organizational credibility.

## Making the Next Moves

Once trained, SPCs have the knowledge, skills, and resources needed to educate and train managers, teams, and other stakeholders to drive the change effectively.
