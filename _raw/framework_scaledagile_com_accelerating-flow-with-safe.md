Accelerating Flow with SAFe

When we start thinking about ways to line up all of the essential steps needed to get a job done into a steady, continuous flow, it changes everything.

– James P. Womack and Daniel T. Jones, Lean Thinking [1]

Definition: Flow is a state that occurs when there is a smooth, linear, and fast movement of work product from step to step in a value stream.

Summary

Establishing and improving flow is how companies sustain business agility. Implementing SAFe creates a flow-based delivery system and fosters a culture that supports it. Value Stream Management builds on this by promoting Lean thinking throughout the organization. 'Make value flow without interruptions' is a principle of SAFe, with eight practices that identify and enable the resolution of flow issues at all levels of SAFe. Additionally, ongoing coaching from experienced Lean-Agile leaders and practitioners accelerates the delivery of value.

What is flow?

Flow is the smooth transition of work through the entire value stream. It reduces handoffs, delays, and rework. SAFe considers flow to be present when teams, trains, and the portfolio can quickly, continuously, and efficiently deliver quality products and services from trigger to value. Today's markets gravitate to value without hesitation. To survive, businesses must provide continuous value through products and experiences that engage and delight customers. Building innovative solutions is not enough to thrive in the digital age. It must be done quickly, and that requires flow.

Figure 1. Flow represents a smooth transition of work through the entire value stream

Flow demands that all individuals and teams in the value stream synchronize around activities that create value. Otherwise, interruptions develop that impede delivery. Indeed, flow is so critical that SAFe uses six metrics to measure how efficiently an organization is delivering value:

Flow Distribution – The proportion of each backlog item type in the workflow
Flow Velocity – The number of backlog items completed in a given time
Flow Time – Time spent from when a backlog item enters the workflow to its release
Flow Load – Total work in process (WIP) across all steps in the workflow
Flow Efficiency – The amount of time backlog items are actively worked on as a percentage of the total time elapsed
Flow Predictability – Overall planned versus actual business value

Understanding flow and the metrics used to measure it allows organizations to optimize their value streams to gain competitive advantages continually. Companies need to spot and react to business opportunities and risks faster than their competitors. SAFe enables this through the Business Agility Value Stream (BAVS). It allows businesses to create innovative solutions quickly while providing the most value possible.

The key to business agility is establishing a smooth and efficient flow by sensing an opportunity to deliver the right solution. This requires optimizing all activities, teams, and events for speed and quality.

How does implementing SAFe establish flow?

Organizations that use SAFe embrace a Lean-Agile way of thinking. They focus on finding and delivering value and organize work to enhance flow. To do this, they prioritize tasks based on cost-effectiveness, develop projects regularly, and release products to center on customer needs.

Adopting SAFe means setting up a system that helps deliver work efficiently by improving flow. For many organizations, this is a big change from older project-based methods.

As they make this change, organizations gain competence across areas of discipline in SAFe:

Team and Technical Agility – The Lean-Agile principles and practices that high-performing Agile Teams and teams of Agile Teams use to create superior solutions
Product Delivery – A customer-centric approach to defining, building, and releasing a continuous flow of valuable products and services
Large Solution Integration and Delivery – Applying Lean-Agile principles and practices to the specification, development, deployment, operation, and evolution of the world's largest and most sophisticated software applications, networks, and cyber-physical systems
Lean Portfolio Management – Aligning strategy and execution by applying Lean and systems thinking to strategy and investment funding, portfolio operations, and governance across value streams

As these areas are mastered, unnecessary activities are removed, and operations are streamlined.

How do Agile Teams establish a flow-focused culture?

Value Stream Management (VSM) is a leadership and technical discipline. It maximizes the flow of business value through the end-to-end solution delivery life cycle. VSM is the foundation of flow-based thinking in SAFe and is rooted in the following five Lean thinking principles:

Specify value by specific product
Identify the value stream for each product
Make value flow without interruptions
Let the customer pull value from the producer
Pursue perfection

These principles foster a shared, Lean-Agile mindset that encourages the entire organization to streamline delivery. Each Lean principle is supported by specific SAFe practices that enable smooth flow. Applying Lean thinking principles unlocks the potential for unprecedented speed.

Enable flow with Agile Team methods

In SAFe 6.0, two new articles help the teams achieve a higher flow of value. SAFe Team Kanban guides teams to apply Kanban as their primary method. In addition to differing activities, this article describes the essential Kanban system teams implement to visualize and measure flow from their backlog to the customer. However, using a Kanban system isn't just for Kanban teams. The SAFe Scrum 6.0 article also guides Scrum teams to apply a similar Kanban system. In addition, the new Scrum Master/Team Coach role is responsible for improving flow for every team, no matter the team's method of choice.

Make value flow without interruptions

Step 3 in lean thinking is about improving flow. To deliver value effectively, organizations need to clear away anything that stops this flow. This means they have to regularly check their value streams for problems, find the causes, and make specific improvements. SAFe Principle #6, which focuses on making value flow smoothly, explains how this flow works and lists eight ways to help accelerate it.

Visualize and limit WIP
Address bottlenecks
Minimize handoffs and dependencies
Get faster feedback
Work in smaller batches
Reduce queue length
Optimize time 'in the zone'
Remediate legacy policies and practices

Each flow accelerator is meant to tackle a specific delivery challenge. Since challenges can arise at any point in the value stream, they are applicable at all levels of SAFe. Experienced Agilists use these accelerators to identify and resolve flow issues, helping the portfolio move toward continuous value delivery. Applying accelerators thoughtfully and consistently is crucial to maximizing delivery efficiency in SAFe.

How do Agile Teams, ARTs, and the portfolio accelerate flow?

SAFe is a system that helps organizations adopt a Lean-Agile mindset, maintain a steady work rhythm, and use DevOps practices. It brings teams together from different areas and encourages everyone to take responsibility for continuously delivering value. When teams and tools work well together, they can achieve remarkable levels of flow.

To achieve this, teams need to find and fix workflow interruptions in different situations. The eight flow accelerators should be used regularly at all levels of SAFe to ensure that value is delivered to customers without delay. Each level applies these accelerators in its own way to help achieve this goal.

Team Flow – A team's success relies on its ability to reliably deliver value to customers, stakeholders, and other teams. While Agile Teams aim to be self-sufficient and work well together, they can still face interruptions that affect their flow. Common problems include bottlenecks caused by insufficient 'T-skills,' individuals not wanting to show incomplete work to fast feedback, local optimization, and excessive technical debt.
ART Flow – Interruptions to flow can hamper an Agile Release Train's (ART's) ability to deliver integrated solutions with speed and quality to its customers. Team challenges can build up and produce larger bottlenecks at the ART level. Large batch sizes can delay customer feedback. Clunky handoffs can delay integration. Legacy governance practices can delay deployment.
Solution Train Flow – Coordinating multiple Agile Release Trains (ARTs) and suppliers and the complexity of large systems can lead to new interruptions. It's difficult to test these big systems fully, and getting quick customer feedback becomes harder. Long lists of future requirements can make it challenging to be flexible and responsive to changes. Outdated rules can slow down the system from the beginning.
Portfolio Flow – Many types of interruptions can affect a SAFe portfolio's ability to deliver a continuous flow of epics, value stream KPIs, and business objectives. Busy leaders find it difficult to spend 'time in the zone' to focus on this critical work. Early feedback on intangible, emergent concepts may also be difficult to gather. Lack of alignment can bottleneck critical decision-making.

Resolving flow issues simultaneously at each level reveals interruptions. Flow improves across the system and continually speeds up value delivery. Of course, someone must be on hand to promote and manage flow acceleration. In SAFe, the responsibility falls to coaches who have or develop the skills to help diagnose and accelerate flow.

What is involved in coaching flow?

Accelerating flow is a specialized skill. It requires expert practitioners throughout the company who can guide teams, ARTs, Solution Trains, and portfolios to optimize the value stream continually.

Coaching flow involves these activities at all levels of SAFe:

Facilitating value stream mapping – Value stream mapping is an essential skill. It visualizes the end-to-end delivery process and measures efficiency. This creates a shared understanding of how work circulates through the organization and where flow needs to be improved.
Establishing Kanban systems – A Kanban system supports every backlog in SAFe. Its pull-based method of regulating flow promotes high throughput and quality.
Measuring flow – As described earlier, SAFe's flow metrics allow an organization to monitor, evaluate, and improve the efficiency of its value streams.
Optimizing flow with the eight accelerators – The pursuit of perfection motivates an organization to accelerate flow and strive for zero delays in the value stream. This requires constant attention to the natural impediments and interruptions to flow.
Cultivating a flow mindset – Applying flow practices and tools produces sustainable, long-term improvement. However, this only happens if the new way of operating becomes a mindset and permeates the organization's culture.

SAFe Practice Consultants (SPCs) often lead the effort. As change agents and stewards of Lean-Agile practices, they move quickly through all levels of SAFe to detect and resolve systemic flow issues. In addition, Scrum Masters, Release Train Engineers (RTEs), Solution Train Engineers (STEs), the Value Management Office (VMO), and the Lean-Agile Center of Excellence all have a role in coaching the organization. They all participate in the process of relentlessly accelerating flow.
