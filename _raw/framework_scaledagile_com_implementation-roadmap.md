# SAFe Implementation Roadmap

> Many leaders pride themselves on setting the high-level direction and staying out of the details. But big picture, hands off leadership isn't likely to work in a change situation, because the hardest part of change—the paralyzing part—is in the details.
> Any successful change requires a translation of ambiguous goals into concrete behaviors. To make a switch, you need to script the critical moves.
>
> —Chip & Dan Heath, Switch [1]

NOTE: This is the home page for the SAFe® Implementation Roadmap series. There are an additional 13 articles in this series.

**Definition:** The SAFe Implementation Roadmap consists of an overview graphic and a 14-article series that describes a strategy and an ordered set of activities for successfully implementing SAFe.

Achieving business agility and the benefits of Lean-Agile development at scale is not a trivial effort, so SAFe is not a trivial Framework. Before realizing SAFe's rewards, organizations must embrace a Lean-Agile Mindset and understand and apply Lean-Agile principles. They must identify their Portfolio(s), Value Streams, and Agile Release Trains (ARTs), implement their Lean-Agile Portfolio(s), build quality in, and establish the mechanisms for continuous flow of value delivery and DevOps. And, of course, the culture must evolve as well.

The transformation of an organization towards full business agility requires the change leadership acumen to create persistent people practices and the mindset needed to support the change. Such a change also requires a practical and proven guide that brings the change into sequential action supported by a robust guiding coalition. Based on proven organizational change management strategies, the SAFe Implementation Roadmap graphic and article series describes the critical moves an enterprise can take to implement SAFe in an orderly, reliable, and successful fashion. The strategy for Implementing SAFe is based partly on John Kotter's work in organizational change management. Kotter identifies eight 'accelerators' of leading a change:

- Create a sense of urgency
- Build a guiding coalition
- Form a strategic vision
- Enlist a volunteer army
- Enable action by removing barriers
- Generate short-term wins
- Sustain acceleration
- Institute Change

## Details

The SAFe Implementation Roadmap steps include:

01. Reaching the Tipping Point
02. Train Lean-Agile Change Agents
03. Create a Lean-Agile Center of Excellence
04. Train Executives, Managers, and Leaders
05. Lead in the Digital Age
06. Organize Around Value
07. Create the Implementation Plan
08. Prepare for ART Launch
09. Train Teams and Launch ART
10. Coach ART Execution
11. Launch More ARTs and Value Streams
12. Enhance the Portfolio
13. Accelerate
