# Strategic Investment Planning (formerly Participatory Budgeting)

> Without involvement, there is no commitment. Mark it down, asterisk it, circle it, underline it. No involvement, no commitment.
>
> —Stephen Covey \[1\]

Note: Introduced in SAFe 5.0, Participatory Budgeting (PB) established a collaborative technique for gathering diverse insights to inform portfolio funding decisions. As the Lean Portfolio Management (LPM) discipline has matured, the SAFe community has continuously refined its approach to dynamic, value-driven funding. Reflecting this evolution, PB is transitioning to **Strategic Investment Planning**. This updated approach preserves the powerful collaborative forums and group negotiations of PB, while establishing stronger financial accountability for Portfolio Leadership and executive fiduciaries. This article describes the Strategic Investment Planning event and will continue to provide the latest proven guidance to our community. It details how outcomes from its collaborative forums provide the objective, data-driven insights necessary for Strategic Portfolio Reviews to make optimal funding decisions. While this transition occurs, courseware and other SAFe materials may still refer to Participatory Budgeting.

**Definition:** Strategic Investment Planning (formerly called Participatory Budgeting (PB)) is a collaborative process for allocating the portfolio budget to its value streams. The updated term for PB is Strategic Investment Planning.

The [organization](https://framework.scaledagile.com/enterprise/) provides a portion of its total budget to each portfolio. In turn, [Portfolio Leadership](https://framework.scaledagile.com/portfolio-leadership) allocates the portfolio Budget to individual products, solutions, and value streams. These value streams fund the people and resources needed to achieve the current portfolio vision and roadmap. Empowered Agile Release Trains (ARTs) advance Solutions and implement Epics approved by Portfolio Leadership.

Strategic Investment Planning (formerly called PB) is a dynamic, collaborative portfolio event. The event enables Portfolio Leadership to gather data and build the consensus needed to invest in the best possible products, solutions, and initiatives. The event is also used to establish or update Lean value stream budgets. Strategic Investment Planning engages a diverse group of business and technical leaders and other stakeholders in the decision-making necessary to establish and adjust value stream budgets on a regular cadence.

Lean Portfolio Management utilizes established [Lean Budget Guardrails](https://framework.scaledagile.com/guardrails/) to provide the right mix of investments to address both near-term opportunities and long-term strategy. These guardrails also ensure that significant investments are approved and that the appropriate level of investment is allocated to technology, infrastructure, and maintenance. These guardrails, along with OKRs and KPIs, promote decentralized decision-making and execution while providing the necessary oversight.

Applying Strategic Investment Planning to establish SAFe Lean budgets has several benefits:

- Allows the portfolio to adjust budgets to support rapidly changing customer and market needs
- Provides leaders with insights and perspectives from multiple stakeholders about existing solutions and proposed epics
- Creates alignment and buy-in on difficult funding choices, improving employee engagement and morale
- Increases ownership of budgets and results in more realistic and achievable budgets than those imposed top-down
- Improves information sharing and knowledge between leaders and teams
- Promotes horizon thinking for portfolio longevity

## 1\. Preparing the Content

### Business Context

The first step in preparing the content is to establish and share the business context. This context typically includes the current state of the business, Strategic Themes, and Portfolio Vision. This information is critical for preparing the investment opportunities to bring to the event and for guiding participants' decisions.

### Total Portfolio Budget and Guardrails

The total portfolio budget is a critical input to the Strategic Investment Planning process. It describes the money available to fund the value streams within a portfolio. The Lean budgets and guardrails guide how the value streams spend money and influence the mix of investment opportunities brought into the event.

### Baseline Solution Investments

Stakeholders need to understand the current investment context to budget responsibly. Each value stream budget has two parts. The first part, baseline solution investments, is the cost to develop, support, and operate a solution that delivers current business capabilities. The second part will be described in the following section, proposed solution initiatives.

## 2\. Assemble the Participants

Strategic Investment Planning is most effective when diverse and responsible representatives are involved. Each role brings a unique and valuable perspective to the decision-making process. Attendees typically include:

- Portfolio Leadership
- Product and Solution Management
- Epic Owners
- Enterprise and Solution Architects
- Business Owners
- Other relevant stakeholders, such as finance, leaders from other portfolios, marketing, or sales

## 3\. Conduct the Forums

Strategic Investment Planning follows a schedule. Participants are organized into groups of five to eight people with various roles and people from different Value Streams. Each group gets the total portfolio budget equally divided among the participants. Each group debates and collaboratively funds the BSIs and PSIs.

## 4\. Analyze Results

The aggregated results of the Strategic Investment Planning forums are analyzed to identify suggested funding patterns. While the event results do not directly determine the budget allocations for the value streams, they provide critical information and input that LPM needs to make an informed decision that maximizes the portfolio's business outcomes.
