# SAFe Overview

SAFe (Scaled Agile Framework) is a comprehensive body of knowledge for implementing Lean-Agile practices at enterprise scale. It synthesizes Agile, Lean, systems thinking, DevOps, and value stream management into an integrated system of proven practices. For thousands of global organizations and government agencies, SAFe has become integral for adapting to change, building resiliency, and thriving as a modern, digital business.

The SAFe tagline is **"Work Differently. Build the Future."** — the idea that the entire organization, from marketing and finance to security and HR, must embrace the same Lean-Agile mindset and practices to align strategy to execution and meet customer expectations.

## The Five SAFe Disciplines

The SAFe Big Picture organizes all knowledge into five disciplines, each addressing a distinct set of business challenges:

| Discipline | Focus |
|---|---|
| [[SAFe-lean-portfolio-management]] | Strategy, investment, governance at portfolio level |
| [[SAFe-team-and-technical-agility]] | Agile team practices, engineering quality |
| [[SAFe-product-development-flow]] | Customer-centric product delivery, design, flow |
| [[SAFe-large-solution-delivery]] | Coordinating complex multi-ART solutions |
| [[SAFe-leadership-and-culture]] | Leadership mindset, transformation, AI, change |

Each discipline is composed of competencies that address specific business problems organizations face.

## The Four SAFe Configurations

SAFe scales from small to very large organizations through four configurations:

1. **Essential SAFe** — The minimal, simplest starting point. Focuses on one or more Agile Release Trains (ARTs) delivering solutions. Excludes portfolio and large solution concerns. The ART is the heart of Essential SAFe.
2. **Large Solution SAFe** — Adds coordination across multiple ARTs via the Solution Train, for organizations building large, complex systems.
3. **Portfolio SAFe** — Adds Lean Portfolio Management for organizations that need to align strategy with execution across multiple value streams.
4. **Full SAFe** — The complete configuration combining all levels: team, ART, large solution, and portfolio.

## SAFe Foundation

Every SAFe configuration includes a shared foundation:

- **Lean-Agile Mindset** — The five principles of Lean Thinking combined with the Agile Manifesto.
- **[[SAFe-core-values-and-principles]]** — Four core values (alignment, transparency, respect for people, relentless improvement) and ten immutable Lean-Agile principles.
- **Lean-Agile Leadership** — Leaders who drive and sustain organizational change by empowering individuals and teams.
- **Continuous Learning Culture** — Values and practices that increase knowledge, competence, performance, and innovation.
- **Implementation Roadmap** — A step-by-step guide for adopting SAFe (see [[SAFe-implementation-roadmap]]).

## Essential SAFe: The Heartbeat

The Agile Release Train (ART) is the primary value delivery mechanism in SAFe. Key characteristics:
- A long-lived, virtual team of Agile teams (5–15 teams, typically 50–125 people)
- Self-organizing and self-managing, spanning functional and geographic boundaries
- Delivers on a Planning Interval (PI) cadence, typically 8–12 weeks
- Produces an integrated, working solution each PI

**Ten Critical Success Factors for Essential SAFe:**
1. Lean-Agile Principles as the guide for all decisions
2. Real Agile Teams and Trains — fully cross-functional
3. Cadence and Synchronization — common iteration rhythm
4. PI Planning — connects strategy to execution
5. Customer Centricity, DevOps, and Release on Demand
6. System Demo — objective evidence of progress every iteration
7. Inspect and Adapt — structured reflection and improvement every PI
8. IP (Innovation and Planning) Iteration — buffer, innovation, and planning time
9. Architectural Runway — technical foundation that enables fast feature delivery
10. Lean-Agile Leadership — executive commitment to the new way of working

## SAFe 6.0: Key Changes

SAFe 6.0 (announced 2023) introduced six primary themes:

1. **Strengthening Business Agility Foundation** — Added Business Agility Value Stream (BAVS), revised core values, retired the House of Lean (tenets absorbed into principles and values), updated Implementation Roadmap.
2. **Empowering Teams and Clarifying Responsibilities** — Clearer role descriptions, responsibility wheels for ARTs, updated key collaborations.
3. **Accelerating Value Flow** — Defined eight flow properties and eight flow accelerators; added Team Flow, ART Flow, Solution Train Flow, Portfolio Flow articles; renamed SAFe Team Kanban; renamed Value Management Office from APMO.
4. **Business Agility Across the Whole Business** — Five new patterns: Business-Enabled ART, Agile Business Train, Agile Executive Team, Agile Business Function, Combined Portfolio.
5. **AI, Big Data, and Cloud** — Added AI, Big Data, and Cloud as first-class elements on the Big Picture.
6. **Measure and Grow and OKRs** — Renamed metrics to Measure and Grow; added OKRs for strategic alignment, epic outcomes, and transformation goals.

**Terminology changes in SAFe 6.0:**
- Program Increment → Planning Interval (PI)
- SAFe Program Consultant → SAFe Practice Consultant (SPC)
- Program Backlog → ART Backlog
- Daily Standup → Team Sync
- ScrumXP → SAFe Scrum
- Pre-PI Planning → Pre-Plan; Post-PI Planning → Coordinate and Deliver

## Key Structural Elements

| Element | Description |
|---|---|
| Agile Team | Cross-functional group of ≤10 people; define, build, test, deploy |
| ART | 5–15 Agile teams; delivers one or more solutions |
| Solution Train | Multiple ARTs + suppliers coordinating a large solution |
| Portfolio | Collection of development value streams aligned to strategy |
| Planning Interval (PI) | Fixed timebox (~8–12 weeks) for ART delivery |
| Iteration | Fixed 2-week timebox for team delivery |
| System Demo | Integrated ART output demonstrated every iteration |
| Inspect & Adapt (I&A) | PI-end event for reflection and improvement |
