# SAFe AI and Emerging Topics

SAFe 6.0 added Artificial Intelligence (AI), Big Data, and Cloud as first-class elements on the Big Picture, reflecting their central importance to how modern enterprises build and operate solutions. The Leadership and Culture Discipline includes "Building an AI Organization" as a core competency.

## Artificial Intelligence (AI) in SAFe

**Definition:** AI is a term used to describe a wide range of smart machines capable of performing tasks that typically required human intelligence. AI can be applied at all levels of SAFe to build intelligent customer solutions, automate value stream activities, and improve customer insights.

### Why AI Matters for SAFe Organizations

Conventional software solutions execute predefined rules for well-understood problems. AI addresses the most complicated scenarios — those with parameters too complex or variable for rule-based systems. Applications today include:
- Consumer services (loan applications, online shopping, scheduling)
- Fraud detection and money laundering prevention (banking)
- Government citizen services
- Military and national security systems
- Healthcare diagnostics and scheduling
- Autonomous vehicles and robotics

AI extends existing solutions to make them more valuable and scalable. More importantly, it enables entirely new solutions and capabilities that create competitive differentiation.

### AI in the SAFe Context

AI applies at every level of SAFe:
- **Portfolio level** — AI-driven investment analysis, strategic theme formation, portfolio prioritization, dynamic financial forecasting
- **ART level** — AI-assisted product management (market research, roadmap planning), architecture design recommendations
- **Team level** — AI for code assistance, test generation, flow metrics analysis, sprint planning

**Building AI-enabled solutions** requires the same Lean-Agile practices as any other software:
- Customer centricity to understand AI use cases that deliver real value
- Continuous Exploration to validate AI hypotheses
- Incremental development — start with an MVP, validate the benefit hypothesis
- Built-in quality including Responsible AI practices
- DevOps and CDP practices adapted for AI/ML model training and deployment

## AI-Augmented Workforce

**Definition:** An AI-augmented workforce exists when employees use organizationally approved commercial or custom-built AI tools to optimize their work.

An AI-augmented workforce lets employees focus on what humans do best — creativity, judgment, teamwork — while AI handles repetitive tasks, data analysis, and pattern recognition.

### Role-Specific AI Use Cases

| SAFe Role | AI Use Case | Key Benefit |
|---|---|---|
| Agile Teams | Domain-specific AI augmentation | Specialized support based on role context |
| Agile Teams | Flow metrics analysis | Identify bottlenecks and process problems automatically |
| Scrum Master/Team Coach | Meeting automation (notes, actions) | Focus on improving team interactions, not administration |
| Product Owner | Backlog prioritization | Analyze customer data and market trends to inform sequencing |
| Product Manager | Roadmap planning | Analyze dependencies and resource availability |
| Release Train Engineer | Optimized PI Planning | Analyze team capacity and past performance data |
| System Architect | Architecture design | Suggestions to improve system designs |
| Business Owner | Strategic decision-making | Understand market trends and financial performance |
| Epic Owner | Epic decomposition | Break large initiatives into manageable feature sets |
| Portfolio Leadership | Dynamic financial forecasting | Adjust funding between value streams based on outcomes |
| SPC | Performance benchmarking | Insights to enhance organizational Agile practices |

**Large Language Models (LLMs)** such as SAFe CoPilot, ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini, and others enable users to access world knowledge through prompts for answering questions, organizing information, analyzing data, and creating content.

### Five Steps to Build an AI-Augmented Workforce

1. **Identify AI tools and use cases** — Focus on tools that enhance productivity and align with organizational goals
2. **Ensure Responsible AI use** — Set policies for data privacy, bias reduction, and ethical standards before broad deployment
3. **Measure and monitor AI impact** — Set success metrics for productivity, quality, and innovation improvements
4. **Invest in upskilling** — Provide training on both technical skills and strategic thinking for AI use
5. **Foster a culture of innovation** — Encourage experimentation with AI; celebrate learning from both successes and failures

### Pitfalls to Avoid

- **Burnout from unrealistic expectations** combined with insufficient training and support
- **Insufficient Responsible AI guidelines** — employees using AI without authorization pose data security risks
- **Overreliance on AI output** — The "human in the loop" principle is essential; AI provides the best value augmenting human judgment, not replacing it

## Responsible AI

**Definition:** Responsible AI refers to the practice of designing and managing AI systems that are trustworthy, explainable, and human-centric.

### Why Responsible AI is Essential

AI introduces specific risks not present in conventional software:
- **Bias** — Training data bias produces biased AI outputs
- **Hallucination** — AI generates outputs that are factually incorrect or nonsensical
- **Data leaks** — Employees entering confidential information into external AI tools expose company and customer data

### The Three Pillars of Responsible AI

**1. Trustworthy AI**
- Privacy — Customer and company confidential information doesn't leak
- Security — Protection against hackers compromising systems
- Resilience — Ability to handle attacks and self-repair
- Reliability — Works as designed when needed
- Accuracy — Provides correct information and results

**2. Explainable AI**
- Transparency — Clear understanding of how AI systems work
- Interpretability — AI decision-making is understandable to humans
- Accountability — Organizations are responsible for AI behavior and outcomes

**3. Human-centric AI**
- Safety — AI doesn't pose dangers to humans
- Fairness — Treats all people equally without bias
- Ethics — Follows moral principles and values society holds
- Inclusiveness — Considers the wide range of potential users
- Sustainability — Does not harm the environment
- Compliance — Follows existing laws, regulations, and standards

### Responsible AI in SAFe Practice

Responsible AI is an executive-level concern — typically initiated by C-level leaders with board-level advisory groups.

SAFe roles and practices support Responsible AI:
- **Strategic Themes** communicate Responsible AI priorities throughout the organization
- **Portfolio leaders** manage Responsible AI governance in investment decisions
- **System Teams** incorporate Responsible AI automated tests into the DevOps pipeline
- **ARTs** include Responsible AI experts in solution development
- **Agile Teams** create NFRs that embed Responsible AI in features
- **Procurement** applies Agile contract principles to ensure Responsible AI qualities in AI provider agreements

## Building an AI Organization (L&C Competency)

SAFe 6.0's Leadership and Culture Discipline includes "Building an AI Organization" as a competency addressing the business problem: "We lack a cohesive, scalable strategy to leverage AI across our organization, leaving us vulnerable to faster-moving competitors."

Key dimensions:
- **AI strategy** — Developing a coherent portfolio-level AI strategy aligned to business objectives
- **AI governance** — Responsible AI policies, oversight structures, and compliance
- **AI skills development** — Upskilling teams and leaders to work with and direct AI systems
- **AI integration into value streams** — Embedding AI capabilities into product development and business processes
- **AI measurement** — Defining how AI investments will be measured for business impact

## Big Data

**Definition:** Big Data addresses portfolio-level data strategy, investment, and governance.

Big data concerns are managed at the portfolio level because they require vision, investment, and governance that spans value streams. Key governance concerns:
- **Availability** — Data is accessible when needed
- **Usability** — Data is in a form that supports the intended use
- **Integrity** — Data is accurate and trustworthy
- **Security** — Data is protected from unauthorized access and misuse

Effective data governance ensures data is consistent, trustworthy, and not misused — directly enabling better business decisions and AI system quality.

## Cloud

**Definition:** Cloud capabilities have fundamentally changed how digitally-enabled solutions are built, deployed, and maintained.

Cloud is a first-class element in SAFe's Big Picture because it fundamentally affects how ARTs build and operate solutions:
- **Infrastructure as Code (IaC)** — Cloud infrastructure provisioned programmatically, enabling Agile operations
- **Microservices and containers** — Enable independent deployment and scaling of solution components
- **Serverless computing** — Developers focus on code without managing infrastructure
- **Cloud-native development** — Designed for scalability, fault isolation, and continuous delivery
- **Multi-cloud and hybrid cloud** — Enterprise platforms spanning multiple cloud providers

Cloud capabilities directly support the Continuous Delivery Pipeline by enabling automated provisioning, elastic scaling, and near-instant deployment environments.

## Developing Sustainable Products

SAFe recognizes that sustainable development — considering environmental impact — is increasingly important for organizations building products and solutions. Sustainability considerations:
- Environmental impact of cloud infrastructure and data centers
- Energy efficiency in solution design and operation
- Supply chain sustainability for hardware components
- Product end-of-life considerations
- Regulatory compliance with emerging environmental standards

Sustainable product development is incorporated into the definition of "sustainable" in SAFe's framework for desirable, feasible, viable, and **sustainable** solutions.
